Our Love Story

An Epic
Love Story

Cliff & Susan Peiffer, Terranora, NSW

Scroll
Terranora, NSW, 2026

This is the story of two people, a terminal diagnosis, a palace in India, and a decision to build something that would outlast every reason not to. It took forty years of love, a trip to the Mughal Empire, and seven years of construction. This is the long version.

Chapter 01 The Meeting

A love that never stopped growing.

Cliff met Susan forty years ago. There was nothing complicated about it: it was just right. They married, had four children together (Wade, Amber, Brad, and Brandy) and built a life as full and loud and loving as any life could carry. They had no idea yet that the hardest chapters were still ahead. Or that they would face every one of them together.

Chapter 02 2009: The Diagnosis
“Three to six months. That was the prognosis. He was forty seven years old.”

In 2009, Cliff was diagnosed with terminal Amyloidosis, a rare protein disorder that systematically shuts down the body’s organs. The doctors were unambiguous: get your affairs in order. He did not. Sixteen years later, he is still here. Still building. Still impossible to kill. The words tattooed on his arm, in Hindi, say it plainly. He means every word.

Chapter 03 August 2010: India

A palace built for love, 450 years ago.

In August 2010, Cliff and Susan travelled to India. At Fatepur Sikri, the ghost city carved from red sandstone in 1571 by the Mughal Emperor Akbar, they walked through the palace built for Jodha Bai, Akbar’s beloved Hindu wife. Standing inside those arches, something became clear: here was a love story built into stone. A home that had survived four and a half centuries. They flew home with photographs, dimensions, and a dream that had no right to be possible.

Chapter 04 2015 to 2022: The Build

Seven years. Every arch, every stone, theirs.

Construction began in 2015. Working with their designer and draftsman Richard, Cliff and Susan took on the role of owner builders: no developer, no shortcut, no compromise. They set about replicating the Jodha Bai Palace on a hillside in Terranora, New South Wales. Every column, every window, every carved detail faithful to the original. In 2021, mid build, Amyloidosis returned aggressively. Multiple Myeloma, bone cancer, was diagnosed. In 2024, early stage Parkinson’s was added to the list. Cliff kept building. Seven years of not stopping, not giving in, not giving up.

Chapter 05 The Retreat

A 450 year old dream, on an Australian hillside.

Jodha Bai opened its doors to guests not long after the last stone was laid. What had begun as an impossible idea between two people, a private vision that defied every reasonable objection, became something the world could share. If you have stayed here, you have slept inside a love story: forty years of partnership, sixteen years of stubborn survival, and seven years of building something that was never supposed to exist. We are glad you found it.

Cliff Peiffer

Founder & Owner, Jodha Bai
